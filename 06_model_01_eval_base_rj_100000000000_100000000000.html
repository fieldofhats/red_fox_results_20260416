6.2 Model interpretation guide

NoteROPE (region of practical equivalence)

ROPE is used to assess whether an estimated effect is practically distinguishable from zero. In this analysis, ROPE is applied to coefficients on scaled covariates, so values near zero correspond to negligible standardized effects.

p_rope represents the proportion of the full posterior distribution that lies within the ROPE.

As a decision guide:

  • p_rope ≥ 0.975: effect is likely not meaningfully different from zero (practically equivalent)
  • 0.025 < p_rope < 0.975: effect is ambiguous or weakly supported
  • p_rope ≤ 0.025: effect is likely meaningfully different from zero

These thresholds are interpretive guidelines and should be considered alongside uncertainty intervals and ecological context.

6.2.1 Convergence (R̂)

R̂ (Gelman–Rubin statistic) assesses MCMC convergence across chains. Values close to 1 indicate good mixing and convergence.

  • R̂ ≈ 1.00: good convergence
  • R̂ > 1.01–1.05: potential convergence issues

6.2.2 Derived quantities

  • p_dat: estimated detection probability summarized by year
  • psi_dat: estimated occupancy probability summarized by year

These summaries provide an interpretable scale for model results and help link parameter estimates to ecological patterns over time.
